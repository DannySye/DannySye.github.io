HON. KATUSIIME ANNET MUGISHA OUTLINES 2026-2031 PLAN OF ACTION FOR BUSHENYI

Eiraka Ryaitu

Hon. Katusiime Annet Mugisha, the reigning Woman Member of Parliament for Bushenyi District, has rolled out her action plan or manifesto for the 2026-2031 term of office. She is among the people who have already expressed interest in contesting in the 2025-26 election and representing her constituency in the 12th Parliament in the 2026-2031 term of office.

Hon. Katusiime has served one term, and her footprint is so satisfying that she has the highest chances of retaining her seat. No serious candidate has stood tall to challenge her candidature, at least in the National Resistance Movement Party primaries, though we so far don’t know what will happen after the primary elections.

The holder of a Master of Business Administration, a Bachelor of Social Development and Counselling, a diploma in primary education, and a grade 3 teachers’ certificate has been on a mission to enable development, unity and well-being for all people of Bushenyi District as part of Uganda. Her footprint is traced in 2017 when she embarked on community-based projects supporting the people of Bushenyi District to live transformed lives, envisioning a united and prosperous Bushenyi District.

Improving Household Incomes and Food Security, Children Education, Spirituality Development, Health and Hygiene, Water, Environment, Electricity, Infrastructure Development and Maintenance have been guiding her BAMUGISHA community development agenda and will continue to be the pillars of economic development for the Bushenyi Woman Member of Parliament (2026-2031).

Hon. Katusiime’s plan of action for 2026-2031 is based on the need to learn lessons from past achievements, consolidate achievements, and use the experience gained to do more for the people of Bushenyi District, among others. Her plan is generally to follow up and monitor the implementation of the NRM Manifesto (2026-2031) in Bushenyi District and specific focus areas that include her personal efforts.

PLAN OF ACTION 2026-2031

HOUSEHOLD INCOME IMPROVEMENT

Following up and monitoring Government Wealth Creation Programmes, Provision of a coffee bursary by distributing coffee seedlings to farmers, Provision of poultry bursaries by hatching and distributing chicks to interested farmers, focusing mainly on youths, and continuing to support village saving groups (VSGs).

HEALTH

Following up and monitoring government health centres to ensure that they are well equipped and have essential medicines and medical personnel who are effective in their work, lobbying and following up upgrading of selected health centres in accordance with government policy, in conjunction with Bushenyi district local government, working with the district leadership to focus on reduction of incidences of maternal and neonatal mortality deaths by lobbying for improved ambulance services, equipping maternal wards, sensitisation through radio programmes and improved antenatal services.

EDUCATION

Here, the MP feels concerned about the issue of salary disparities among teachers. She will continue to advocate and dialogue for equitable salaries among teachers, improving the status of school infrastructure, scholastic materials, and adequacy of competent teachers in some parts of the district, which is currently not good. In conjunction with Bushenyi DLG, Hon. KAM will continue to lobby and advocate for a sufficient, decent, and enabling study environment for learners in schools, with adequate scholastic materials, good classrooms, and competent teachers.

WATER AND ELECTRICITY

Knowing all the networks to work with to serve her people, Hon. KAM will continue working on increasing water and electricity coverage in the Bushenyi District. For electricity, she will follow up on sub-counties that are not served and are not yet mapped. At the same time, she will follow up on electricity extension to the sub-counties that are already mapped. For piped water, she will advocate for an additional water treatment plant for Bushenyi District. She will also advocate for the construction of a sewerage system for Bushenyi-Ishaka Municipality.

ROAD INFRASTRUCTURE

She will continue to advocate for the rehabilitation of Ishaka-Mbarara Road and the tarmacking of Nsiika-Nyakabirizi roads that are already under design/consideration by the government. In conjunction with the Bushenyi-Ishaka Municipal (BIM) Council, the woman MP will advocate for improved BIM roads through the USMID Project. Through the District Roads Committee, she promises to continue to ensure equitable improvement of road infrastructure in the district.

YOUTH EMPOWERMENT

Hon. KAM will continue assisting the youths who have relevant academic qualifications to be treated fairly in accessing opportunities for formal employment but also continue to sensitise all youths on the opportunities that lie in self-employment. Where possible, she will advocate for competence-based training of youths to obtain relevant skills and also lobby for mindset change training for youth in different sub-counties.

FUNDRAISING SUPPORT AND DONATIONS

Where possible, Hon. KAM will continue to support churches, mosques, schools, and community organisations in their action-orientated fundraising efforts as she has been doing.

LEGISLATION, OVERSIGHT AND APPROPRIATION

As expected, the MP will play her primary roles, which derive, largely, from business presented by the executive in Parliament. She will also work to enhance her perfection in performing these roles by selecting committees of Parliament that largely incorporate her manifesto focus areas, strengthening comradeship and stakeholder networks, exploring benchmarking opportunities inside and outside Uganda and ensuring that the welfare of Ugandans is paramount.

This plan of action (manifesto) highlights what the woman MP did before she became District Woman Member of Parliament (2017-2021), during her term as MP (2021-2025), and the promises for 2026-2031.

SUMMARY OF ACHIEVEMENTS

ACHIEVEMENTS MADE 2017-2021

Before she was elected Woman MP, Hon. KAM worked with different groups to start women and men saving groups at every village, worked with youths to start saving groups at every parish in the whole district, worked with leaders and coordinators of BCWL to set up other saving groups, through different radio programmes and other forums, sensitized the community the need to GROW FOOD FOR HOME USE and EXTRA INCOME, Pioneered the setting up of Bamugisha Community Welfare Beneficiaries SACCO in Bushenyi for sustainable support to community saving groups, fundraised for over 70 tonnes of maize flour supplies to vulnerable communities during COVID-19 pandemic period among others.

ACHIEVEMENTS MADE FOR 2021-2025

During her first term of office, Hon. KAM lobbied fundraising support and donations, supported cross-cutting lobbying activities, raised salient issues in Parliament, did parliamentary oversight for specific areas in Bushenyi district, and participated in the making of different bills at committee level.
